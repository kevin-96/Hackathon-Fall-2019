For this reason, student loan balances have ballooned across the country. There is a total exceeding 1.5 trillion in student loan balances nationally. By becoming more informed about your options when paying for school, these figures can begin to come down and impact less american lives.

Credit Card Debt

Tips

  • Pay off your entire credit card balance every month to avoid interest charge
  • If you cant pay off your total balance, try to pay as much as possible to decrease charges
  • Pay on time to avoid late fees
  • Understand your APR(Annual Percentage Rate) so that you can estimate interest
  • Try to pay for purchases you know you can pay for
  • Check the fee schedule with your issuing bank to avoid unnecessary fees
  • Pay attention to credit card rewards and use them when possible

What is a Credit Card?

Credit cards are cards issued by banks that allow you to pay for purchases on credit. Credit cards have a credit limit which is the maximum balance you can carry on a card.

Credit Cards vs. Debit Cards

Credit card transactions involve borrowing money from the bank to complete a purchase. The bank determines a maximum amount that it is willing to lend (credit limit) and approves or declines a transaction based on account standing. Debit card transactions involve a checking account. Transactions made with debit cards are based on a bank account. They use money already allocated by the account holder and transactions are approved or declined based on that account balance.

What is a Mortgage?

Morgages are the loans that allow people to purchase a house and pay for it over a set amount of time. Unlike other loans, if defaulted on, lenders are able to take your house if there is non-payment.

Why do I need one?

In many cases, a home purchaser does not have the amount of money it costs to purchase a house. By getting a mortgage, they would not have to wait years to ammass the money required to purchase that house. While they allow for homes to be purchased earlier in life, there are also financial risks involved with mortgages. Payments could change if interest is variable.

Possible Expenses

The costs of owning a home go beyond paying for the actual house. There are also insurance costs to protect against emergency spending. Utility bills are also a consideration as rates can vary depending on location. Tax rates can also vary across town lines, further adding costs to a house. Check and make sure you know the details before commiting to a home.
